\begin{abstract}
We study a framework for generating task-specific neural network weights from small support sets using a support encoder, a hypernetwork decoder, and a diffusion model over latent solver codes. A deterministic encoder produces strong amortized solvers, while diffusion learns a stochastic distribution over plausible task-specific solutions. On toy classification tasks, diffusion mainly improves best-of-k selection rather than one-shot accuracy. On regression tasks, diffusion provides modest gains on held-out families and larger gains under best-of-k evaluation.
\end{abstract}
